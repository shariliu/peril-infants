# Sensitivity to risk in infants' intuitive psychology

## Overview

Infants assume that agents plan over variables like physical effort and reward (Liu et al., 2017). This research asks whether infants consider the risks agents plan over as well. Here, risk is defined as degree of danger, or the magnitude of a negative state that an agent might encounter (falling down a cliff).

In one set of experiments, we ask whether infants expect agents to minimize the risk of their actions, all else being equal. We do this by showing infants an agent choosing a high-risk over a low-risk action for the same reward, and vice versa, and measuring their looking times to assess their expectations.  

In another set of experiments, we ask whether infants infer the value of a goal from the amount of risk an agent took for it.

## Design: Minimizing Risk

**Trial Structure**: 2 pre-fam 1, 1 pre-fam 2, 6 fam, 4 test

**Counterbalanced Factors** 
1. First test event (low vs high risk) 
2. First scene configuration during test (LH vs HL)
3. First fam (fam1 vs fam2)
4. Prefam 1 scene configuration (LH vs HL) 
5. Prefam 2 scene configuration (L or R)
5. Prefam first event (H or L)

**Number of conditions**: 4

| Cond | First test | First scene config during test | First fam | Prefam 1 scene config | Prefam 2 scene config | Prefam first event
| ---- | ---------- | ------------------------------ | --------- | --------------------- | --------------------- | -----------------
| 1    | Low        | LH                             | fam1      | HL                    | L                     | High
| 2    | Low        | HL                             | fam2      | HL                    | L                     | High
| 3    | High       | LH                             | fam2      | LH                    | R                     | Low
| 4    | High       | HL                             | fam1      | LH                    | R                     | Low
